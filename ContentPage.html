The Quality of Education Around the World

The United Kingdom

Quality of education in the United Kingdom
The United Kingdom possesses a valuable status in terms of its quality in education. This had been evidenced in expatica for internationals report, where it stated that in the 2018 PISA tests, the UK performed above the OECD average for reading, Mathematics and Science. Additionally in primary and secondary schools, advanced teaching techniques are implemented by certified academic professionals that aim to harness the youth into educational prosperity. With demanding and strenuous examinations such as GCSEs and A-Levels being undertaken by the youth, it is undoubtable that the quality of education in the UK is outstanding. Furthermore, universities in the UK such as Cambridge, Oxford, UCL and many more, hold a huge global reputation, where people from the UK as well as people worldwide compete to gain prospect in these educational institutions. Therefore, it is clear that the quality of education in the UK is exceptionally high.

The United States of America

Quality of education in the United States of America
Like the United Kingdom, The United States of America holds a valuable quality in education, where Ivy League universities such as MIT, Harvard, Princeton and many more, possess a grand global reputation, where people all across the globe strive to study in these exceptional educational institutions to further succeed in their career paths. Imed Bouchrika in his 2024 article on US Public Education Spending stated that according to the Report on the Condition of Education in 2021, the total expenditure of the USA on elementary and secondary education between 2017 and 2018, amounted to $762 billion. Hence, with an arena of successful and prospective universities as well as high investment into education, it is evident that the USA's quality of education is outstanding.

India

Quality of education in India
According to Unicef India's reports, the quality of education in India is relatively poor due to many classrooms being continued in being subjected under teacher-centred rote learning, corporal punishment and discrimintation. Moreover, the Education in India Wikipedia pagestated that regardless of more than 95% of children attending primary school, only 40% of Indian adolescents attend secondary school. ClearIAS concluded as of March 2024, that India's literacy rate is 74%, with Kerala topping the rankings, followed by Delhi, Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu. Bihar is the lowest amongst the Indian states. Despite these setbacks, India boasts some extremely difficult exams such as the Joint Entrance Examination, where it rigorously challenges engineering applicants in their journey in being accepted into India's best engineering universities such as the Indian Institutes of Technology and the National Institutes of Technology. Thus, with this mixed educational record, it can be judged that India's quality of education is somewhat moderate but possesses exceptional features, particularly in higher education.

Afghanistan

Quality of education in Afghanistan
Gender equality in education, especially for women, in Afghanistan has been the centre of discussion for quite a while. After the Taliban's seizure of Afghanistan in 2021, Islamic education has been implemented as well as women face a great deal of challenges in order to gain equal access to education. The UN Human Rights Office Of The High Commissioner heavily highlighted in 2023 that quality education must be equally accessible to all. The perpetual unlawful denial of girls and young women from their right to education in Afghanistan showcases why we need to do more. In consequence of this factor, an entire gender and the future generation of the world is greatly impacted. If you join the cause for greater quality education, you would be aiding the silent victims, who have been denied their basic right of an education. Volunteering and contributing to the cause is the only thing required. Globally and for the essence of humanity, our goal is to improve the lives of everyone around us.

Somalia

Quality of education in Somalia
The quality of education in Somalia can be characterised by unpleasant circumstances. The Borgen Project reports that there is a low primary school enrollment rate, where only 30% of children on average attend school, and only 18% attend in secluded regions. There is a vast gender disparity, where girls are provided way less access to education than boys. The blog continues to elaborate that Somalia possesses high rates of female genital mutilation (FGM), with 98% of Somali girls undergoing FGM at some point in their lives, with 80% of them being between ages 5-9. Due to the Somali civil war that consequently occurred in 1991, this has resulted in a lack of educational resources and impacted the nation drastically. It is clear that one country's quality of education would be better than the other country. This is due to unchangeable circumstances for the unfortunate countries. With your donation and involvement in our cause, you would be ensuring in greater quality education for young children worldwide.
